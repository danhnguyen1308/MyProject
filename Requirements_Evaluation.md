# Requirements Evaluation Guide

This document outlines the process for reading user-input requirements and evaluating their complexity.

## Reading User Requirements

1. **Collect Input**: Gather all requirements provided by the user through various channels (e.g., forms, emails, meetings).
2. **Document Clearly**: Record the requirements in a structured format, such as bullet points or numbered lists, to ensure clarity.
3. **Verify Understanding**: Confirm with the user that the captured requirements accurately reflect their needs.

## Evaluating Complexity

Complexity can be assessed based on several factors:

- **Scope**: The breadth and depth of the requirements.
- **Dependencies**: Interconnections with other systems or components.
- **Technical Challenges**: Novel technologies, integrations, or constraints involved.
- **Timeline**: Urgency and available resources.
- **Risks**: Potential issues like security, scalability, or compliance.

### Complexity Levels

- **Low**: Straightforward tasks with minimal dependencies.
- **Medium**: Moderate scope with some technical challenges.
- **High**: Complex projects requiring extensive planning and resources.

Use this guide to systematically analyze and prioritize requirements.
